The 2008 Mumbai Attacks

Coordinated terrorist attacks that shook India and the world

Overview of the Attacks

The 2008 Mumbai attacks, also known as the 26/11 attacks, were a series of coordinated terrorist attacks carried out by 10 members of Lashkar-e-Taiba, who executed 12 shooting and bombing attacks across Mumbai from 26 to 29 November 2008. The attacks resulted in 175 deaths, including nine of the attackers, and more than 300 people were injured.

Key Attack Locations

  • Times of India Building and St. Xavier's College
  • Mazagaon, Mumbai's Port Area
  • Vile Parle Taxi Explosion

Attack Timeline

The attacks unfolded over four days, beginning on 26 November 2008 and ending on 29 November 2008. The National Security Guards (NSG) played a key role in ending the attacks, culminating in Operation Black Tornado, which neutralized the last of the attackers at the Taj Hotel.

The Attackers

Ajmal Kasab, the sole surviving attacker, was captured and later executed in 2012. He confirmed that the attackers were part of the terrorist group Lashkar-e-Taiba and were controlled from Pakistan. The Pakistani government later acknowledged his identity. Key figures such as Zakiur Rehman Lakhvi and Sajid Majeed Mir were involved in the planning and funding of the attacks.
